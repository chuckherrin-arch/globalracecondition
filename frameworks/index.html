Analytical Frameworks

Global Race Condition uses multiple analytical lenses to examine geopolitical developments. No single framework explains everything. The value is in applying several simultaneously and watching where they converge, diverge, or fail. When independent frameworks predict the same outcome from different causal chains, that convergence is the highest-signal finding.

These are the primary frameworks referenced in the briefings. Each page provides the historical background, core concepts, and application rules that inform the analysis.

The American Technate

A 1930s movement that mapped a self-sufficient continental zone from Greenland to Panama. Its 1940 map almost exactly mirrors current US territorial ambitions. The energy-based economic model has unexpected resonance in the age of AI compute demands.

Dugin's Russian Strategic Framework

Aleksandr Dugin's "Foundations of Geopolitics" (1997), the textbook taught at Russia's General Staff Academy for two decades. A published strategic doctrine for Russian imperial restoration through anti-Atlantic fragmentation.

Eschatological Lenses

Religious end-times frameworks that shape decision-making in the current conflict: Shia Mahdism within the IRGC, Christian Zionism in the US evangelical base, Jewish religious Zionism in Israel's political right, and their practical implications for de-escalation calculations.

These frameworks are analytical tools, not endorsements. A framework can be useful for pattern-matching observed behavior even when its original authors had agendas of their own. The test is predictive accuracy, not ideological purity.
