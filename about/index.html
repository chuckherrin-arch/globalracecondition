Chuck Herrin

CISSP, CCSP

I spent over two decades protecting some of the world's largest enterprises from threats they didn't see coming. As a CISO, CTO, and board member across financial services, insurance, and technology, I've learned that the most dangerous risks are the ones leadership doesn't know about yet.

The Global Race Condition newsletter is how I share that lens publicly. Multiple times per week, I synthesize signals across geopolitics, cybersecurity, AI, quantum computing, and macroeconomics into analysis that helps business and technology leaders make better decisions. No vendor spin, no hidden agenda; just the signal that matters along with the context to understand why.

I also research the biological constraints that drive human conflict at scale, work I call "Parenting a Young God", exploring how evolutionary limits on fear processing and social cognition create predictable failure modes in organizations, markets, and geopolitics.
