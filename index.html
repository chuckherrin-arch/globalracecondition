1. WHAT MOVED TODAY

Two US Combat Aircraft Down. The Stalemate Geometry Just Cracked.

An F-35 and an A-10 Warthog both went down on Friday, with the F-35 crew status still unconfirmed (search and rescue ongoing) and the A-10 pilot rescued. Iran Dispatches reports this is the second claimed F-35 loss (IRGC claimed one on March 19; CENTCOM denied it). The A-10 loss is confirmed by Middle East Eye.

The significance is not the loss of two airframes. It is that the loss of US pilots in Iranian territory or Iranian-controlled waters introduces a new variable into the endgame calculation. A missing F-35 pilot with two crew members unaccounted for transforms the political geography of negotiation. Iran can now offer prisoner release as a bargaining instrument. The US domestic tolerance for war costs: already thin per polling: faces a new test the moment families see that Americans are missing and potentially captured. That the war had achieved tactical objectives and none of the strategic ones. That assessment remains true. The addition of captured or missing personnel shifts when the political ceiling on US prosecution of the war arrives, not whether it arrives.

The Selective Hormuz Regime Now Has Explicit Mechanism

Friday’s development adds critical detail to the morning brief’s observation that Iran is running a discriminatory blockade. Three Omani-operated tankers, a French-owned container ship, and a Japanese LNG vessel all transited on April 2-3. Al-Monitor now reports explicitly that Iran said it would permit transits by ships with no US or Israeli links.

This is not a leak or rumor. This is Iran’s stated operational policy. That France and Japan’s neutrality was being rewarded. The evidence now shows Iran has constructed a nationality-based access framework in real time. The mechanism: flag-of-convenience vessels register under neutral states, cross through with Iranian clearance, then operate under their true beneficial ownership. This is a working port system, not a rhetorical stance. The practical implication: Iran has converted a military chokepoint into a diplomatic one. Every country now faces the calculus of whether its vessels’ ownership structure and beneficial ownership align with neutrality as Iran defines it. This accelerates decoupling from the US-led coalition faster than any sanctions threat could.

The $1.5 Trillion Defense Request Includes $350B in Reconciliation Bypass

Responsible Statecraft provides the structural detail that changes the political calculus: $1.15 trillion comes through the normal budget process; $350 billion is designated for reconciliation: a process that bypasses the Senate’s 60-vote threshold and requires only 51 votes.

This is not a procedural detail. This is a signal about what Congress expects to happen if the war continues. Its invocation suggests that Pentagon leadership has already calculated that the normal budgeting process cannot move fast enough for what the Iran war will cost. This is the equivalent of triggering emergency protocols before the emergency is declared. Combined with the $200 billion supplemental for the Iran war specifically, the message to markets is: the US government is prepared for a long, expensive conflict and is structuring its financial commitments accordingly. The Technate thesis (deliberate hemispheric consolidation) struggles here. The Dugin framework (fracture US-led alliances and force internal division) predicts exactly this: spiraling defense costs, alliance strain over burden-sharing, and domestic political fragmentation as Americans watch the war consume resources. By this measure, the war is performing according to the Russian strategic playbook.

Pope Leo Enters the War as a Religious Authority Figure

Al-Monitor reports that Pope Leo led a candle-lit Good Friday service in the Colosseum featuring prayers for war orphans and deported immigrant children, and an explicit warning to world leaders that their decisions will be judged by God. That religious framing was operating as one factor among several in the conflict. The Pope’s intervention elevates that factor into institutional authority.

The eschatological dimension is now operating at the highest level of the Catholic institution. When the head of the global Catholic Church uses the central site of Christian martyrdom to address a war and place it within a theological frame of divine judgment, that carries weight with two audiences simultaneously: the US evangelical base that supports the war (Christian Zionism), and European Catholics who may recoil from having their faith hierarchy explicitly oppose their governments’ military posture. This is not a marginal voice. This is institutional Christianity signaling that the war violates theological commitments that bind believers across national boundaries.

The Ceasefire Rejection Is Now Official

Iran’s Foreign Ministry formally rejected a US proposal for a 48-hour ceasefire, according to Al-Monitor and Middle East Eye reporting. Iran’s reported stance that it would not tolerate cycles of war, negotiation, ceasefire, and repetition.

The formal rejection confirms that this is not a negotiation stalling tactic. It is a refusal to engage in the de-escalation framework Washington is proposing. Iran’s counter-demand. US withdrawal from all Middle Eastern bases and compensation for infrastructure destruction: is incompatible with the US political position. The gap between what Iran demands and what the US will offer is now explicitly stated. No middle ground is visible. This confirms the morning assessment that the stalemate has no clear exit and increases the probability of either a prolonged conflict or an escalation that forces one side to accept terms.

European Leadership Abandons Messaging Discipline

Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni flew to the Gulf region unannounced on Friday, visiting Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and UAE. Middle East Eye notes she is the first EU or NATO leader to visit the region since the war began. Her stated mission: reaffirm support for Gulf states and boost Italian energy security.

This is a crack in NATO messaging discipline. Meloni is allied with Trump and conducts independent diplomacy. Her unannounced visit to the region signals to both Iran and the Gulf states that Europe is fragmenting its response. France negotiated passage for its ships through the Strait. Japan secured the same. Now Italy is conducting bilateral energy diplomacy with Saudi Arabia and Qatar. That selective Hormuz passage was fragmenting Western coalition unity. European capitals are now accelerating that fragmentation through independent action. The signal to adversaries: do not count on Western unity.

If the F-35 crew remains unaccounted for beyond dawn Saturday: Watch for Iran to announce prisoner claims or release demands.
